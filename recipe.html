Cajun Salmon with Lemon Dill Cream

(Click here to step through preparation instructions.)

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 4

This recipe is something I often enjoy as a staple since, while it is rich and flavourful, it is still light and versatile. In colder seasons, enjoy with steamed greens and roast vegetables; in warmer times of year, pair with a nice refreshing salad and fresh bread.

Ingredients

Here's a list of what you'll need. Once you've got an ingredient ready, click on it to check it off the list!

4 4oz. portions fresh salmon fillet
1 to 2 lemons
1/2 cup shallot, minced
175ml 35% whipping cream
175ml white wine
1.5 tbsp fresh dill weed, chopped (substitute dried dill if required)
1 tbsp Cajun seasoning
salt & pepper

Preparation

    Salmon (30 minutes)

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Oil an oven safe, shallow dish and place fillets skin-side down with an inch of space between.
  3. Season evenly with salt, pepper, Cajun seasoning, and half of your chopped dill weed. Rub lightly into flesh.
  4. Zest lemon, setting lemon zest aside, covered. Cut lemon lengthwise and into 1/4" slices ("sunrise" cut).
  5. Lay lemon slices in a layer atop fillets, providing coverage from direct heat. If possible, overlap slices to eliminate large gaps.

    6. Tip: Looking for something to do earlier in the day? Prepare the filets to this point ahead of time! Simply cover and refrigerate until 30 minutes before meal time.

  6. Once oven has reached temperature, bake uncovered for 20-25 minutes, or until cooked medium.

    7. Sauce (10 minutes)

  7. Heat a saucepan over medium heat. Add white wine, bring to boil, and then simmer over low heat for 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. Add whipping cream slowly into pan, stirring steadily. Do not allow cream to boil.
  9. Add shallot, lemon zest, and the remainder of dill weed. Salt and pepper to taste. Simmer until thickened, stirring constantly.

    10. Tip: Not sure when the sauce is ready? Trust the wooden spoon! A wooden spoon is ideal for testing a cream sauce's consistency because of its grain. Dip the back of the spoon in your sauce and hold it over the pan - does the sauce coat the spoon or just run off? If it coats the spoon, you're all set!

  10. Add salt, pepper, white wine or lemon juice to taste. Once satisfied with the flavour, remove sauce from heat and allow it to thicken on standing (~2 minutes).

Plating

  1. Remove lemon slices from surface of filets. Set aside.
  2. Lift filets (with skin) onto plates, careful not to break or shred meat.
  3. Drizzle filets with cream sauce.
  4. Garnish each plate with baked lemon slices and a sprig of fresh dill and serve.
